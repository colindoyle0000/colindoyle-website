---



| Scaling for Deference | Key                                                          |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| De novo = 0           | A court will never consider the agency’s interpretation in determining the meaning of a statute. |
| *Skidmore* = 1        | A court can consider agency expertise in determining whether they should allow for an agency’s expertise to substitute for its own. |
| *Chevron*-lite = 2    | A stricter version of *Chevron* where courts have leeway in substituting their own interpretations even after finding reasonableness. |
| *Chevron* = 3         | Two-step model of *Chevron*.                                 |
| *Chevron*-plus = 4    | A court is not required to find reasonableness in order to defer. |

Source: Christopher J. Walker & Neena Menon, *Chevron’s 51 Imperfect Solutions*, 2024 Wis. L. Rev. 1585 (2024)
